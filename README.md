Zomato Data Analysis

Introduction

Zomato is a popular restaurant discovery and food delivery platform that provides valuable insights into customer preferences, restaurant ratings, and pricing strategies. This project aims to analyze Zomato's dataset to extract meaningful insights regarding restaurant trends, customer reviews, pricing strategies, and geographic distributions.

Objectives

Understand the distribution of restaurants across different cities.

Analyze customer ratings and their correlation with pricing and cuisine type.

Identify popular cuisines and high-performing restaurants.

Explore the cost-effectiveness of dining in different locations.

Detect trends in user reviews and sentiments.

Dataset

The dataset used in this analysis consists of:

Restaurant names and locations

Ratings and reviews

Cost for two people

Cuisine types

City-wise distribution

Online order and booking availability

Technologies Used

Python: For data processing and visualization

Pandas: To manipulate and analyze data

Matplotlib & Seaborn: For data visualization

NumPy: For numerical operations

Scikit-learn: For clustering and predictive modeling

Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

Handled missing values and duplicate entries.

Converted categorical data into numerical values for analysis.

Normalized price ranges for consistency.

Analyzed text-based reviews using Natural Language Processing (NLP).

Key Insights

Top Cities for Restaurants: Cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore have the highest restaurant density.

Rating vs. Price Analysis: Higher-rated restaurants generally have moderate to high pricing, but exceptions exist.

Popular Cuisines: North Indian, Chinese, and Italian cuisines dominate the market.

Online Orders & Bookings: Restaurants with online ordering and table booking options tend to have higher engagement rates.

Sentiment Analysis: Most customer reviews are positive, with keywords like "delicious," "great service," and "value for money."
